How the score is calculated

All collected signals are normalized to 0–100 range and passed to Claude as structured context. Claude produces a final momentum score with a weighted synthesis:

Score = Σ ( signal_value × weight ) Live: News(25%) + Claude_synthesis Live: + Reddit(15%) + GitHub(15%) + HuggingFace(10%) + PyPI(5%) + Wikipedia(5%) + AppStore(5%) + HN(5%) + Funding(10%) Later: + ArXiv(5%) + HN(5%) + YouTube(5%) + AppStore(5%) + Funding(10%) Range: 0–100 | Neutral baseline: 50 Updated: 07:00 UTC and 19:00 UTC daily

The score reflects momentum, not absolute quality. A company can have great products but score low if there's been no activity. A sudden release or controversy moves the score immediately.

Transparency commitment: Every score update is logged with the source signals that produced it. Historical data is immutable — scores cannot be edited retroactively. If a data source fails or returns anomalous data, the update is skipped and marked with a warning flag visible on the dashboard.
